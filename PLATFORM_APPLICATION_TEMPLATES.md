### Template 1: List Article (High Engagement)

**Structure:**
```
Headline: [Number] [Adjective] [Topic] That [Benefit]
Example: "7 Simple Habits That Transformed My Productivity"

Hook (1-2 paragraphs):
- Start with a relatable problem or surprising statistic
- Promise the solution/value they'll get

Introduction (2-3 paragraphs):
- Your personal story or credentials
- Why this matters
- What they'll learn

Main Content (The List):
Each item should have:
‚Ä¢ Clear subheading
‚Ä¢ 100-200 words explanation
‚Ä¢ Personal anecdote or example
‚Ä¢ Actionable tip

Conclusion (2-3 paragraphs):
- Summarize key takeaways
- Call to action (what should they do next?)
- Personal encouragement

Total length: 1,200-1,800 words
```

**Example Hook:**
```
I used to end every day feeling busy but unproductive. My to-do list never got shorter, my inbox never emptied, and I constantly felt behind.

Then I discovered 7 simple habits that changed everything. In just 30 days, I doubled my output while working fewer hours. Here's exactly what I did.
```

### Template 2: How-To Guide

**Structure:**
```
Headline: How to [Achieve Result] in [Timeframe] [Optional: Without Common Obstacle]
Example: "How to Build a $5,000/Month Freelance Business in 90 Days Without Experience"

Opening:
- What you'll learn
- Who this is for
- What you'll need

Step 1: [Clear Action]
- Why this step matters
- How to do it (specific instructions)
- Common mistakes to avoid
- Time required

Step 2: [Clear Action]
[Same structure]

[Continue for 5-10 steps]

Conclusion:
- Next steps
- Encouragement
- Resources

Total length: 1,500-2,500 words
```

### Template 3: Personal Story/Lesson

**Structure:**
```
Headline: I [Did Something] and Here's What I Learned

- Lessons learned in real-time
```

---

**Pro Tips for All Templates:**
1. Always personalize - mention something specific from their posting/profile
2. Keep it concise - most people skim, not read
3. Show, don't tell - "99% accuracy" not "I'm very accurate"
4. Include a clear call to action
5. Proofread 3 times (typos kill credibility)
6. A/B test different versions to see what works

**When to Use Each:**
- **Short templates**: Simple, straightforward projects
- **Long templates**: Complex projects worth $500+
- **Standing out templates**: Competitive jobs with 20+ proposals
