## üí∞ PRICING NEGOTIATIONS

### Script 1: Client Asks "What's Your Rate?"

**SITUATION**: Client asks your rate before sharing budget

**SCRIPT**:
```
"Great question! My rate depends on the scope and complexity of the project.

To give you an accurate quote, could you share:
- What's the timeline?
- How many [deliverables] do you need?
- What's your budget range for this?

This helps me tailor my proposal to your needs. Most projects like this range from $[low] to $[high], depending on those factors.

Looking forward to discussing further!"
```

**WHY IT WORKS**:
- Doesn't commit to price first
- Shows professionalism (you price per value, not blanket rate)
- Gets them to reveal budget
- Gives a range (anchoring effect)

**VARIATIONS**:

**More Direct**:
```
"I typically charge $[X] for [specific deliverable]. Could you share a bit more about your project scope and budget so I can give you an exact quote?"
```

**For Hourly Work**:
```
"My hourly rate is $[X]. For a project like this, I estimate it would take [Y] hours, so around $[total]. Does that work with your budget?"
```

### Script 2: Client Says "That's Too Expensive"

**SITUATION**: Your quote is above their budget

**SCRIPT - Option A (Flexible)**:
```
"I understand budget is important. Let me ask - what range were you thinking?

Depending on that, we have a few options:
1. We could reduce the scope [explain what you'd remove]
2. We could extend the timeline [explain how that reduces cost]
3. We could do a phased approach [explain priorities]

My goal is to deliver value within your budget. What would work best for you?"
```

**WHY IT WORKS**:
- Shows empathy (you understand)
- Doesn't immediately drop price (maintains value)
- Offers alternatives (problem-solving approach)
- Makes them choose (you're still in control)

**SCRIPT - Option B (Hold Firm)**:
```
"I appreciate you being upfront about budget.

My rate reflects [specific value you provide - speed, quality, expertise]. I've found that [result clients get from your work].

That said, I want to be respectful of your budget. If the rate doesn't work, I completely understand and wish you the best with your project!

If you'd like to revisit this in the future when budget allows, I'd be happy to discuss."
```

**WHY IT WORKS**:
- Reinforces your value
- Shows you're not desperate
- Leaves door open
- Often they come back with more budget

**SCRIPT - Option C (Meet in Middle)**:
```
"I see. My standard rate is $[original], which reflects [value].

Here's what I can do: For this first project, I can offer $[middle ground - maybe 15% less], and we can reassess for future work based on results.

Sound fair?"
```

**WHY IT WORKS**:
- Small concession (not huge discount)
- Framed as special (one-time)
- Conditional (only for first project)
- Builds relationship

### Script 3: When You Need to Raise Rates (Existing Client)

**SITUATION**: You've been working at $X, now worth $Y

**SCRIPT**:
```
Hi [Client Name],

I wanted to give you advance notice about a rate adjustment.

I've really enjoyed working with you over the past [timeframe]. In that time, I've [specific value you've delivered - increased their traffic, saved them time, etc.].

As I've grown my expertise and taken on more clients, I'm adjusting my rates to reflect the value I bring. Effective [date - give 30+ days notice], my rate will be $[new rate] for new projects.

Because I value our working relationship, your current rate of $[old rate] will remain in effect for any projects started before [date].

I'm happy to discuss this if you have any questions. I look forward to continuing our great work together!

Best,
[Your Name]
```

**WHY IT WORKS**:
- Advance notice (respectful)
- Acknowledges relationship
- Justifies increase (value delivered)
- Grandfathers them temporarily (goodwill)
- Professional tone

**VARIATION - For Retainer Clients**:
```
"Hi [Client],

As we approach [renewal date], I wanted to discuss a rate adjustment for the next period.

Since we started, I've [list achievements - 3 specific wins]. The results speak for themselves!

For the next [month/quarter], my rate will be $[new rate], which is [X%] increase and reflects the [complexity/results/market rate].

I believe the value I provide justifies this, but I'm open to discussing if you have any concerns.

Sound good?"
```

### Script 4: Client Wants Discount for "Exposure" or "Future Work"

**SITUATION**: "I can't pay much but you'll get great exposure!"

**SCRIPT**:
```
"Thanks for thinking of me!

I appreciate the opportunity, but I don't work for exposure or promises of future work - I've found those rarely materialize.

What I can offer instead:
- My standard rate of $[X] for full scope
- OR reduced scope for your budget (here's what I can do for $[Y])

Happy to discuss what works best for your budget!"
```

**WHY IT WORKS**:
- Polite but firm
- Doesn't insult them (but sets boundary)
- Offers alternatives
- Maintains value

**VARIATION - When You Actually Want the Project**:
```
"I love the project, but I can't work for exposure alone.

Here's a compromise: I'll do it for $[reduced rate - but not free], which is 50% off my normal rate. In exchange, I'd like:
- A video testimonial when we're done
- Introduction to 3 potential clients
- Full rights to use it in my portfolio

This way we both get value. Work for you?"
```

## üìã PROJECT SCOPE NEGOTIATIONS

### Script 5: Client Keeps Adding Work ("Scope Creep")

**SITUATION**: Project keeps growing beyond original agreement

**SCRIPT - First Time**:
```
"Hi [Client],

I'm happy to [new request]!

Quick note: This is outside the original scope we agreed to, which was [list original scope].

I can definitely handle this. It would be an additional $[amount] and add [timeframe] to the timeline.

Or, if you'd prefer, we can stick to the original scope and handle this as a separate project after.

Let me know how you'd like to proceed!"
```

**WHY IT WORKS**:
- Friendly tone (not accusatory)
- Clarifies scope (reminder of original agreement)
- Offers solution
- Makes them decide

**SCRIPT - Repeat Offender**:
```
"Hi [Client],

I notice we've had several additional requests beyond the original scope:
- [Request 1]
- [Request 2]
- [New request]

I want to make sure I'm delivering what you need, but these additions have expanded the project significantly.

Here are two options:
1. We revise the scope and price to $[new total] to include everything
2. We complete the original scope, then start a new project for the additions

Which would you prefer? I'm happy to accommodate either way, but want to make sure we're on the same page."
```

**WHY IT WORKS**:
- Professional (not frustrated)
- Specific (lists the additions)
- Clear options
- Stands firm

### Script 6: Client Wants Rush Delivery

**SITUATION**: "Can you do this by tomorrow?"

**SCRIPT - If You Can**:
```
"I can make that work!

For rush delivery (under [X] days notice), I charge a 50% rush fee, so this would be $[original price √ó 1.5].

If that works for you, I can start immediately and have it to you by [deadline].

Or, if timeline is flexible, I can deliver by [normal timeline] at the standard rate of $[normal price].

Let me know which you prefer!"
```

**WHY IT WORKS**:
- You can say yes (but at premium)
- Teaches them to plan ahead next time
- Gives them choice

**SCRIPT - If You Can't**:
```
"I'd love to help, but unfortunately I'm committed to other clients through [date] and don't want to compromise quality.

The earliest I could deliver this is [realistic date].

If that doesn't work for your timeline, I completely understand! If it does, I'm happy to get started right away."
```

**WHY IT WORKS**:
- Honest
- Protects quality
- Respectful of their needs
- Doesn't over-promise

### Script 8: Client is Rude/Disrespectful

**SITUATION**: Client uses inappropriate language or tone

**SCRIPT - First Warning**:
```
"Hi [Client],

I'm committed to delivering great work for you, and I expect professional communication from both sides.

I'm happy to discuss any concerns you have, but I need us to communicate respectfully.

Let's reset and continue working together productively. What specific issues can I help address?"
```

**WHY IT WORKS**:
- Sets standard
- Doesn't escalate
- Refocuses on solutions
- Gives them chance to correct

**SCRIPT - If It Continues**:
```
"Hi [Client],

After our previous conversation about professional communication, I've noticed [specific behavior].

I don't work in environments where I'm not respected. I'm going to [complete current work/refund and end engagement].

[If completing] I'll deliver [deliverable] by [date], and then we'll part ways.

[If refunding] I'm issuing a full refund and ending the project effective immediately.

I wish you the best with your project."
```

**WHY IT WORKS**:
- Firm
- Professional (even when they're not)
- Clear consequence
- You're firing them (you're not desperate)

## üß† NEGOTIATION PSYCHOLOGY PRINCIPLES

### 1. **Anchor High**
First number mentioned sets the "anchor"
- If they ask your rate first: Give top of your range
- If they give budget first: Aim for 10-20% above

### 2. **Never Justify Lower, Always Justify Higher**
- ‚ùå "I can do it cheaper because I'm new"
- ‚úÖ "My rate is higher because I deliver X, Y, Z"

### 3. **Make Them Choose**
Don't just say yes or no - offer options
- "We can do A for $X or B for $Y"
- Makes you look flexible
- They feel in control

### 4. **Silence is Power**
After you state your price: SHUT UP
- Don't fill the silence
- Let them respond first
- Whoever speaks first often "loses"

### 5. **Bundle for Perceived Value**
- "4 articles for $400" sounds better than "$100/article"
- "Monthly package" sounds premium vs. "hourly rate"

### 6. **Use "I" Statements, Not "You"**
- ‚ùå "You're asking for too much work"
- ‚úÖ "I can't deliver quality at that scope/price"

### 7. **End with Question**
Forces them to engage
- "Does that work for you?"
- "What would you prefer?"
- "Sound fair?"

## ‚ö†Ô∏è RED FLAGS: When to Walk Away

### These phrases mean trouble:

**"We'll pay more next time"**
‚Üí They won't. Get paid fairly now.

**"Everyone else charges less"**
‚Üí Let them hire "everyone else." Don't compete on price.

**"This will be great exposure"**
‚Üí Exposure doesn't pay bills.

**"We need to see your work first" (free)**
‚Üí Your portfolio is the sample. Don't work for free.

**"My budget is $X" (insultingly low)**
‚Üí "Thanks, but I can't deliver quality at that rate. Good luck!"

**"Can you start now?" (before discussing pay/scope)**
‚Üí "Let's discuss scope and budget first."

### Walk Away If:

- They disrespect you
- They keep changing scope without adjusting pay
- They want spec work (you compete for free)
- They refuse to put agreement in writing
- Your gut says no

**Remember**: There are always more clients. Protect your time and value.

## üéØ FINAL TIPS

### Before Every Negotiation:

1. **Know your bottom line** (what's your walk-away point?)
2. **Know their motivation** (why do they need this?)
3. **Have alternatives ready** (other clients, other options)
4. **Practice out loud** (sounds different than in your head)
5. **Remember**: You're solving their problem (you have value!)

### During Negotiation:

1. **Listen more than talk** (understand what they really need)
2. **Ask questions** (more info = more power)
3. **Take your time** ("Let me check my schedule and get back to you")
4. **Stay professional** (emotion loses deals)
5. **Be willing to walk** (desperation shows)

### After Negotiation:

1. **Get it in writing** (email confirmation minimum)
2. **Clarify everything** (scope, timeline, payment, revisions)
3. **Start strong** (first impression sets tone)
4. **Deliver more than promised** (want them to say "worth every penny")
